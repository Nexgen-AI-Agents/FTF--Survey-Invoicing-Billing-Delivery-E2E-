CHANGE ORDER CLAUSE
NexGen Land Surveying — Estimate Terms & Conditions

All estimates provided by NexGen Land Surveying are based on the scope of work described
at the time of quotation. Acceptance of this estimate constitutes agreement to the
following change order terms.

WHAT CONSTITUTES A CHANGE ORDER
Any request to modify, expand, or reduce the original scope of work after estimate
acceptance is a change order. This includes but is not limited to:

  - Additional boundary lines, corners, or structures not included in the original quote
  - Additional research required due to undisclosed encumbrances, title issues, or
    missing recorded documents
  - Re-visits to the property caused by changed site conditions, inaccessible areas,
    or incomplete client preparation (e.g., property not cleared, locked gates)
  - Revised or additional deliverables (e.g., adding a legal description to a boundary
    survey, adding a flood zone determination to an elevation certificate)
  - Work required due to discovered discrepancies between recorded plats and
    actual site conditions that materially affect scope

HOW TO REQUEST A CHANGE ORDER
  1. Contact NexGen in writing at info@nexgensurveying.com or (561) 508-6272
     before any additional work begins.
  2. NexGen will assess the new scope and provide a written change order quote
     within one (1) business day.
  3. Work on the additional scope will not begin until the client provides
     written approval of the change order quote.

PRICING AND PAYMENT
  - Change order fees are billed at the same service rates as the original estimate.
  - Change orders are invoiced separately and are due upon completion of the
    additional work, unless otherwise agreed in writing.
  - NexGen reserves the right to place the original deliverable on hold pending
    written change order approval if the scope change materially affects the outcome.

LIMITATION OF LIABILITY
NexGen Land Surveying is not responsible for project delays, cost overruns, or incomplete
deliverables caused by scope changes that were not communicated in writing prior to
fieldwork commencement. Verbal instructions to expand scope do not constitute an
approved change order.
