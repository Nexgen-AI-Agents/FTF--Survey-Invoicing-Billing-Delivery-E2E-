## SECTION 2 â€” Florida PSM Regulations

### Licensing Authority
- **FDACS (Florida Department of Agriculture and Consumer Services)** â€” NOT DBPR like most FL professions. This is unique nationally.
- Florida Board of Professional Surveyors and Mappers operates under FDACS

### Chapter 472 F.S. â€” PSM Licensing Requirements

**Pathway 1 (Surveying Degree):**
- Bachelor's or higher in surveying, mapping, geomatics, or related field
- Minimum 4 years experience under licensed PSM in responsible charge
- Degree time does NOT count toward experience

**Pathway 2 (Other Degree):**
- Bachelor's in any field
- 6 years experience under licensed PSM (5 years in responsible charge)
- Minimum 25 semester hours in surveying-related subjects

**Both pathways:** Must pass PSM licensure examination administered by FDACS

**License Renewal:** Biennial (every 2 years); 24 hours CE including at least 6 hours Laws & Rules / Standards of Practice

**Out-of-State:** Must pass Florida's jurisdictional exam regardless of other state licenses

**Workforce reality:** ~2,879 licensed PSMs in Florida (declining from ~3,184). For every 5 retiring, only 1 enters. Shrinking supply + surging construction demand = pricing power for firms that can deliver.

**Business entity:** Must hold Certificate of Authorization (COA) to practice as an LLC/corporation â€” cannot rely solely on PSM employee's individual license number. NexGen Surveying LLC must hold a COA.

### Chapter 5J-17 FAC â€” Minimum Technical Standards

**5J-17.051 â€” General Survey Requirements (eff. 11/13/2017)**
Every survey must display:
- Legal description and boundary markers
- Surveyor's seal, signature, and PSM license number
- Date of survey and field work completion
- North arrow and scale
- All pertinent measurements and calculations
- All easements and encumbrances visible in the field

**5J-17.052 â€” Boundary Survey Requirements (eff. 7/15/2018)**
- All corners must be physically monumented (concrete, steel, wood, or approved materials)
- Each monument must bear PSM license number or COA number
- Initial point must tie to a government corner, recorded corner, or well-established survey point
- When sketch accompanies description: must state "not a survey"
- Water boundary surveys: must describe which feature was located (top of bank, mean high water line, etc.) and the method used

**5J-17.053 â€” Professional Matters**
- Closure tolerances based on survey type and land area
- Instruments appropriate to required precision
- Documentation of measurement methods and equipment

**5J-17.010 â€” Grounds for Discipline**
Negligent/incompetent practice, fraudulent credentials, failure to abide by Standards of Practice, conflicts of interest, practicing with lapsed license, unauthorized practice without COA

**5J-17.062 â€” Electronic Seals**
Florida requires specific procedures for electronically transmitted documents â€” important for NexGen's digital delivery workflow

### Florida vs. Other States â€” Key Differences I Know
1. FDACS (agriculture dept) regulates surveyors â€” unique nationally
2. **Florida Form 9 Endorsement** â€” virtually all institutional lenders require it; title companies cannot issue Form 9 without a current survey. This is why every residential mortgage closing generates a survey order. Without survey: title policy has "survey exception" â€” buyer is exposed for encroachments and boundary disputes.
3. **Datum complexity** â€” Florida bridges NGVD29 and NAVD88 with no universal statewide conversion factor. In Monroe County the average conversion is approximately -1.5 ft, but varies throughout. Old FEMA maps used NGVD29; current FIRMs use NAVD88 â€” mixing them without conversion creates serious errors.
4. **Sovereign submerged lands** â€” Mean High Water Line (MHWL) is the ownership boundary between state sovereignty lands and private uplands. In tidal/coastal properties the MHWL is ambulatory â€” it moves naturally â€” making these boundaries complex and legally sensitive.
5. **Riparian rights** â€” Water boundary disputes are far more common in Florida than inland states.
6. **CCCL (Coastal Construction Control Line)** â€” Established in 25 Florida coastal counties. Construction seaward of CCCL requires DEP permit. Surveyors must locate CCCL for coastal projects. Especially critical in Monroe County.
7. **Miami-Dade High Velocity Hurricane Zone (HVHZ)** â€” Additional documentation requirements beyond standard national practice.
8. **Declining PSM workforce while construction booms** â€” Supply/demand creates real pricing leverage for capable firms.

### Common Violations/Complaints in Florida (I Watch For)
- Inaccurate boundary location (missing monuments, wrong corners)
- Failure to set required monuments
- Failure to disclose water boundary ambiguity
- Mixing NGVD29 and NAVD88 without noting datum used
