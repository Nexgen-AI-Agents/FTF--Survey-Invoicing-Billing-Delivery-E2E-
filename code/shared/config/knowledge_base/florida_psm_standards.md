## Top 10 Technical Standards (5J-17.051)

1. **Accuracy scaled to purpose** — no single universal tolerance; PSM selects appropriate standard per job type; all data subject to independent verification.

2. **Datum must be stated** — horizontal and vertical datum declared on all products with elevation data. Assumed datums permitted only when values are obviously different from published benchmarks.

3. **Adequate control points** — when assumed datums used, establish control points so any qualified surveyor can reproduce and verify.

4. **Mandatory map/report content:**
   - PSM name, license number, certificate of authorization number and address
   - Survey type stated explicitly ("Boundary Survey", "Topographic Survey", etc.)
   - Survey date = last date of data acquisition
   - Revision dates when graphics updated post-delivery
   - North arrow and scale
   - Bearing source referenced
   - Definitions of all surveying abbreviations
   - Responsibility statement for each mapped feature

5. **Record documentation** — all field measurement and computation records dated; sufficient data to substantiate final product; field notes are legal records.

6. **Elevation data** — products showing elevation must state datum and methodology; PSM legally liable for FEMA Form 086-0-33 accuracy on Elevation Certificates.

7. **Pre-survey research (boundary surveys)** — sufficient records research to identify: conflicting record boundaries, ownership locations, abutting boundaries, access issues.

8. **Monument standards (boundary):**
   - Minimum length: 18 inches
   - Minimum cross-section: 0.20 square inches
   - Composed of durable material (iron rod/rebar typical)
   - Magnetically detectable
   - Bear PSM license number or Certificate of Authorization number

9. **Boundary inconsistencies — mandatory disclosure** — overlapping descriptions, conflicting boundaries, encroachments must be noted AND explained on map, never omitted.

10. **Flood zone integration** — most FL county building departments and lenders require flood zone determinations and CCCL data on boundary surveys near water. Adds 1-2 hours labor.

## Boundary Survey Requirements (5J-17.052)

**What must appear on the map:**
- Label "Boundary Survey" explicitly
- Lot/block numbers and subdivision name (platted) or metes-and-bounds (unplatted)
- Course bearings and distances
- Monument descriptions: size, material, cap ID, FOUND or SET
- All easements, rights-of-way, cemeteries, improvements (structures, driveways, pools, fences)
- Water boundary designations: top-of-bank, edge-of-water, or mean high water line
- Any inconsistencies, overlapping descriptions, or potential encroachments — must be noted and explained

## Elevation Certificate Requirements

- Must be prepared by licensed FL PSM or licensed Professional Engineer
- Uses FEMA Form 086-0-33
- PSM certifies: accuracy of measurements AND compliance with FEMA standards
- Key measurements: lowest floor elevation, adjacent grade, garage floor, machinery/equipment elevations vs. Base Flood Elevation (BFE)
- Elevation certificates do NOT expire absent structural changes
- Required for: new construction in SFHA, federally-backed mortgages in flood zones AE/VE/AH/AO
- Each foot above BFE reduces NFIP premiums; 2+ feet above = lowest rates

**Florida Flood Zones:**
| Zone | Risk | Insurance Required |
|---|---|---|
| AE | High — 1% annual chance | Yes |
| VE | High — coastal/wave action | Yes |
| AH/AO | Shallow flooding | Yes |
| X (shaded) | Moderate — 0.2% annual | No |
| X (unshaded) | Low risk | No |

## Accuracy Standards

5J-17 does not specify a single numeric tolerance for all surveys:
- **Boundary surveys**: angular closure 1:7,500+ (residential); 1:10,000+ (ALTA)
- **Elevation data**: FEMA requires 0.1-foot accuracy (0.2 ft acceptable for older benchmarks)
- **ALTA/NSPS**: governed by 2021 joint ALTA/NSPS Minimum Standard Detail Requirements

## PSM License Requirements (Chapter 472, F.S.)

**Standard pathway:**
- Bachelor's in surveying/mapping + 4 years subordinate experience (including responsible charge)
- OR: Bachelor's in other field + 25 semester hours surveying/civil/math + 6 years (5 in responsible charge)

**Alternative pathway (effective January 30, 2026 — HB 339):**
- No degree required: 4+ years experience + licensed PSM employer recommendation
- License expires December 15, 2031 unless 20 semester hours completed

**All pathways:**
- Must pass NCEES Fundamentals of Surveying (FS) + Principles and Practice of Surveying (PS) exams
- Biennial renewal with continuing education
- "Responsible charge" = direct control and personal supervision
- Seal/electronic signature requirements in 5J-17.060 and 5J-17.062

**PSM certification covers:**
- Work performed under direct supervision
- Measurements accurate to stated standard
- Work meets applicable MTS
- For elevation certificates: measurements meet FEMA standards

## Florida vs. Other States — Key Differences

| Factor | Florida | Typical Other State |
|---|---|---|
| Flood zone | Nearly mandatory near water; CCCL required coastal | Rarely on boundary surveys |
| Monument spec | 18" min, 0.20 sq in, magnetically detectable | Varies; many states no size mandate |
| Adverse possession | Does NOT recognize fence-line alone; strict statute | Many states allow fence-line |
| Elevation certificate | Required for new construction in SFHA | Less common |
| CCCL surveys | Required coastal construction; FDEP regulated | No equivalent most inland states |
| Deed conflict | Mandatory notation AND explanation on map | Often PSM discretion |
| Regulatory body | FDACS (not standalone board) since 2013 | Most states have standalone board |
| 2026 licensing | New non-degree pathway Jan 30, 2026 | Most states still require degree |

## NexGen Quoting Implications

- FL boundary surveys near water: add flood zone research → +1-2 hours labor
- South FL (Miami-Dade, Broward, Palm Beach): 15-25% cost premium vs. North FL
- CCCL work: FDEP coordination required → separate line item
- Missing monuments: common in older FL subdivisions → budget $100-400/corner for setting
- Form board surveys: required by virtually all FL counties → recurring revenue
- Monroe County (Florida Keys): among highest-cost in state; tidal/CCCL complexity
- Panhandle: lowest cost region; at or below state average
